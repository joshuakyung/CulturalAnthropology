Ethnocentrism — William Graham Sumner (1906)

SLIDE 3
Definition

"The tendency to view your own cultural group as the center of everything — and to use your own cultural standards as the measure of all others."

Not arrogance — a default Unconscious Universal
🧱

Field Cases — Indonesia

SLIDES 5–6
Case 1 · Methodological Ethnocentrism
"The Disciple with a Knife"
Direct public correction (Korean default) → public shame (Indonesian high-context reality). Method ≠ culturally neutral.
Case 2 · Surface Success / Hidden Failure
"The Worship Transplant"
Rigid program structure in relational community. Attendance ≠ engagement. Hiebert: surface success is the most dangerous failure.

Neo-Ethnicity — Three Axes of Fragmentation

SLIDES 8–10
Core Claim

One ethnic/national group ≠ one culture. New cultural differentiation emerges within groups along three axes.

Axis 1
Geography
Urban ↔ Rural
Axis 2
Social Class
Income & Education
Axis 3
Generation
Digital Natives ↔ Elders
Key Term

동시대의 비동시대성 — People sharing the same calendar year but inhabiting different lived cultural eras.

Worldview — Definition & Iceberg

SLIDE 3
Definition

The set of fundamental assumptions through which a person interprets reality, self, others, time, morality, and ultimate meaning — usually without knowing it.

Above waterline
Behavior / Language
What we see first
Below waterline
Values / Norms
Shapes behavior
Deepest layer
Worldview
The operating system

Case 1 — Moral Grammar: Guilt vs. Shame

SLIDE 5
Guilt–Innocence
Shame–Honour
Wrongdoing experienced as
Legal violation
Loss of face / contamination
Core question
"Am I guilty?"
"Am I clean? Do I belong?"
Gospel resonance
Acquittal / pardon
Cleansing / restoration / adoption
Common contexts
Western, Korean
South / Southeast Asian, Indian caste
Missiological Point

The problem is not theological — it is translation. The Gospel addresses both frameworks. Scripture contains both legal acquittal and cleansing, restoration, adoption.

⏱️

Case 2 — Time Orientation

SLIDE 6
Linear-Time
Time = Resource
Punctuality = respect · West / Korea
Event-Oriented
Time = Container
Event begins when community gathers · South/SE Asia
Field Story · Java
"The 10am Service"
Pre-service fellowship = the liturgy · Korean missionary missed it
Source

Lingenfelter & Mayers, Ministering Cross-Culturally — time orientation as a worldview clash, not a discipline problem.

💬

Case 3 — High / Low Context Communication

SLIDE 7
Low-Context
Meaning in words
Explicit · Direct · Germany / USA
High-Context
Meaning in context
Indirect · Relational · Japan / Indonesia / India
Source
Edward Hall
Beyond Culture — high/low context spectrum
Field Story A · Indonesia
"The Missing Clothes"
Direct accusation = irreparable shame. Indirect correction preserved face and restored the situation. Silence ≠ dishonesty.
Field Story B · Indonesia
"The Phone Bill"
"This month's phone bill... wow." — entire message delivered without a single direct word. High-context = richer, layered frequency.
